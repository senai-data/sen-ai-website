Tab restructure: 16 flat to 3 honest

The 16-tab strip is the highest-impact target. It overflows on every screen below 1440px and treats a YouTube check and a compliance export as peers. Here is the honest regroup, the working 2-level nav, and the top-3 user flows.

The problem

Sixteen equally-weighted tabs in one horizontal strip. It scrolls sideways, hides the last 5 behind an overflow, and gives no sense of where am I in the workflow.

The honest regroup

I rejected the strawman's 8-tab "Investigation" bucket - it breaks Miller (5-7) and lumps off-site sources with owned-property audits. My grouping splits on user intent, not data type, and moves Competitors where it belongs.

Share of voice is a measurement, not an investigation. An agency owner reads it next to her mention rate, so it lives beside Citations.

Sources splits Off-site vs Owned

Reddit and Wikipedia are surfaces you influence indirectly. Page Audit, Schema and Internal links are things you directly fix. The sub-label keeps the honest distinction without a 4th macro.

Crisis leads Act

Serial position - the most urgent leaf sits first and carries the only coloured dot. Everything else in the strip stays quiet until it needs eyes.

The working nav

A macro segmented control over a contextual leaf row. Click a macro to swap its leaves; click a leaf to load. Max visible choices at once stays within Hick's comfortable range.
