Deliverable 5 of 5 - Mobile

Mobile strategy

A clear call on where responsive effort goes, defended in three lines, with the graceful small-screen fallback for the in-app surfaces that stay desktop-class.

DECISION - OPTION C (HYBRID)
Mobile-first marketing & auth. Desktop-class app with a graceful mobile gate.
One. The money decisions - read a landing page, register, verify email, reset a password, accept an invite - happen on phones, so the marketing and auth tier is fully mobile-first.
Two. The in-app dashboards are dense, multi-tenant, audit-grade tables built for triage across 30 brands - cramming a 12-column share-of-voice grid onto 375px would lie about the data, so they stay desktop-class at ≥1024px.
Three. Below 1024px the app does not 404 or break - it shows a calm "continue on a wider screen" gate that still surfaces the one thing a phone user needs: which brands are in crisis, with a deep link to email themselves the report.

Why not the other two

The brief offered (a) full mobile-first everywhere and (b) desktop-only with a dead-end gate. Both lose.

OPTION A - rejected
Full mobile-first, every surface
A 16-column citations table has no honest 375px form - you either hide columns (data loss) or scroll-trap the user.
Doubles the layout surface to QA across 35 pages before paying clients arrive. Wrong order of work.
OPTION B - rejected
Desktop-only, hard dead-end
An agency owner gets a crisis email on her phone, taps through, and hits a wall. That is the worst possible moment to fail.
Marketing & registration also break on mobile - killing the top of the funnel.
